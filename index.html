Questions, answered

Not for a straightforward Will. In the UK you can make your own Will if it's in writing and signed correctly with the required witnesses. Our service guides you through the wording and the signing steps so a simple estate can be handled without a solicitor. For complex situations (business interests, trusts, foreign property, large IHT exposure), we recommend a solicitor review.
In England & Wales and Northern Ireland you need two independent witnesses, both adults (18+), present at the same time while you sign. They must then sign in your presence. Witnesses (and their spouses/civil partners) should not be beneficiaries — otherwise any gift to them becomes void. They should be of sound mind and able to see the signing happen in person.

In Scotland, one witness is sufficient for a "self-proving" Will; the witness must be old enough (16+) and their name and address are recorded. This helps avoid extra proof later.
Yes — we generate a Scottish-compliant version and provide a tailored signing guide. Under the Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995, a Will that's signed by you and properly witnessed is valid; making it "self-proving" with a witness (and correct details) avoids delays. Many practitioners also advise signing each page as good practice.
